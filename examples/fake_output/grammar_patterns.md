# Recurring Grammatical Patterns & Rules

### 1. Omission of Indefinite Articles Before Professional Roles
- **Example from Transcript:** `"I am backend developer"` / `"I am automation engineer"`
- **Correct Form:** `"I am a backend developer"` / `"I am an automation engineer"`
- **Grammar Rule:** In English, singular countable nouns indicating a person's profession or title MUST be preceded by an indefinite article (`a` or `an`). Use `a` before consonant sounds (`a developer`, `a system`) and `an` before vowel sounds (`an engineer`, `an architect`).

---

### 2. Present Perfect Continuous vs. Present Continuous for Duration
- **Example from Transcript:** `"I working with Python for five years now"`
- **Correct Form:** `"I have been working with Python for five years now"`
- **Grammar Rule:** When describing an action that started in the past and continues without interruption into the present moment (especially when accompanied by `for + duration` or `since + time`), you must use the Present Perfect Continuous tense (`subject + have/has been + verb-ing`).

---

### 3. Missing Direct Objects with Transitive Verbs (`Put / Make / Set`)
- **Example from Transcript:** `"we put in Redis cache"` / `"I make a microservice that process..."`
- **Correct Form:** `"we put it in a Redis cache"` / `"I built a microservice that processes..."`
- **Grammar Rule:** Transitive verbs like `put`, `get`, `make`, and `set` require an explicit direct object (a noun or pronoun like `it/them`) immediately following the verb. You cannot leave the object implied in English as you might in Romance languages.

---

### 4. Third-Person Singular Subject-Verb Agreement (`Doesn't / Processes`)
- **Example from Transcript:** `"so the database don't crash"` / `"that process millions of events"`
- **Correct Form:** `"so the database doesn't crash"` / `"that processes millions of events"`
- **Grammar Rule:** In the present simple tense, third-person singular subjects (`he`, `she`, `it`, `the database`, `the microservice`) require an `-s` or `-es` ending on the verb (`processes`, `runs`) and take `doesn't` instead of `don't` for negative clauses.
