# Actionable 5-Day English Practice Plan (15 Min / Day)

> **Goal:** Eliminate recurring article omissions and present perfect continuous slips while sharpening STAR method delivery for remote USD/EUR technical interviews.

---

### Day 1: Grammar Fundamentals — Articles & Tenses
- [ ] **Exercise 1 (5 mins):** Write down 10 professional sentences using `a` and `an` correctly before job titles (`I am a backend engineer`, `She is an SRE`, etc.). Read them aloud twice.
- [ ] **Exercise 2 (10 mins):** Convert 5 past/ongoing experience statements into clean Present Perfect Continuous syntax (`I have been working with... for X years`).

---

### Day 2: STAR Method & Pacing Practice
- [ ] **Exercise 1 (5 mins):** Take your improved Q2 answer (Database Scaling) and break it into clear mental bullet points (`Situation -> Action -> Result`).
- [ ] **Exercise 2 (10 mins):** Record yourself on your phone or OBS explaining your database scaling answer in under 90 seconds without looking at your notes. Focus on steady pacing.

---

### Day 3: Vocabulary & Technical Phrases Integration
- [ ] **Exercise 1 (5 mins):** Review the `vocabulary_to_study.md` table. Pick 3 terms (`connection pooling`, `decouple`, `proactive optimization`).
- [ ] **Exercise 2 (10 mins):** Answer the mock question: *"Tell me about a challenging architectural bug you solved recently"* ensuring you naturally incorporate those 3 terms.

---

### Day 4: Pronunciation & Third-Person Singular Drills
- [ ] **Exercise 1 (5 mins):** Practice saying third-person singular verbs ending in `-s` or `-es` clearly (`processes`, `runs`, `doesn't crash`, `handles`).
- [ ] **Exercise 2 (10 mins):** Read aloud the 3 `IMPROVED_INTERVIEW_ANSWERS` at a calm, executive pace. Pay attention to enunciating the final `-s` consonants.

---

### Day 5: Full Mock Rehearsal & Self-Evaluation
- [ ] **Exercise 1 (10 mins):** Do a full 3-question mock interview simulation using OBS recording. Answer Q1, Q2, and Q3 as if speaking to a live US engineering manager.
- [ ] **Exercise 2 (5 mins):** Watch your recording. Check off: Did you use `a/an` before titles? Did you say `have been working`? Did you keep a confident, steady tone?
